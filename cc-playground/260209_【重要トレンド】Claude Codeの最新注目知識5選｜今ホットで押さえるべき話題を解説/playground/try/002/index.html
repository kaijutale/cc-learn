The Legacy

A Journey Through Time

1921

The Birth of a Legend

Guccio Gucci, inspired by the elegant luggage he observed while working at London's Savoy Hotel, opens a small leather goods shop in Florence. Drawing on Tuscany's finest artisan traditions, he begins crafting luggage, bags, and accessories that marry impeccable craftsmanship with timeless elegance.

1937–1947

Innovation Through Scarcity

World War II material shortages spark radical innovation. Gucci pioneers the use of bamboo, hemp, linen, and jute as luxury materials. In 1947, the iconic Bamboo Bag is born — artisans heat-treat bamboo cane over open flames, bending it into the bag's signature curved handle. What begins as necessity becomes a hallmark of Gucci ingenuity.

1953

Icons Are Born

A defining year for the House. The iconic green-red-green web stripe — inspired by the traditional girth strap of horse saddles — becomes Gucci's most recognizable motif. The same year sees the introduction of the Horsebit loafer, featuring a gilt metal snaffle bit across the vamp, forever redefining luxury footwear. The interlocking GG logo is registered, creating one of fashion's most enduring symbols.

1961–1969

The Hollywood Glamour Era

Gucci becomes the brand of choice for the world's most glamorous women. In 1961, Jacqueline Kennedy is photographed carrying Gucci's hobo bag — forever after known as the "Jackie." In 1966, Princess Grace of Monaco receives a custom silk scarf adorned with a floral print, giving birth to the legendary Flora pattern. Gucci opens boutiques on Fifth Avenue, Rodeo Drive, and beyond.

1970s–1980s

Global Expansion

Gucci embarks on aggressive worldwide expansion, opening stores across Asia, the Middle East, and major European capitals. The GG canvas becomes a status symbol on every continent. The brand diversifies into ready-to-wear, accessories, and fragrances, transforming from a leather goods house into a full luxury lifestyle brand — though not without internal family turbulence that would later inspire books and films.

1994–2004

The Tom Ford Revolution

Tom Ford is appointed Creative Director, and the world takes notice. His vision of sleek, provocative sensuality — low-rise velvet trousers, satin shirts, razor-sharp tailoring — transforms Gucci from a heritage house into the most talked-about brand in fashion. Paired with CEO Domenico De Sole, revenues soar tenfold. Ford's Gucci defines 1990s glamour and proves that heritage and modernity can coexist in spectacular fashion.

2006–2014

Frida Giannini's Elegance

Frida Giannini takes the creative helm, bringing a softer, more romantic sensibility. She reimagines the Flora print for a new generation, introduces the Bamboo Bag in contemporary silhouettes, and champions the brand's equestrian heritage. Her tenure balances Gucci's storied past with a forward-looking femininity, maintaining commercial success while honoring the codes that made the house legendary.

2015–2022

Alessandro Michele's Maximalism

Alessandro Michele ushers in a seismic shift. His debut collection — an explosion of embroidered silks, gender-fluid silhouettes, vintage eyewear, and eclectic layering — rewrites the rules of luxury fashion overnight. Gucci becomes a cultural phenomenon, embracing inclusivity, historical references, and unabashed maximalism. The Dionysus bag, the GG Marmont, and the Ace sneaker become generation-defining icons. Revenue doubles under his visionary leadership.

2023–Present

Sabato De Sarno — Quiet Luxury

Sabato De Sarno, formerly of Valentino, is appointed Creative Director, signaling a return to refined elegance. His Gucci Ancora collection — named after the Italian word for "still" and "again" — embraces a warm, saturated red palette and pared-back sensuality. The approach is quiet luxury: impeccable tailoring, rich materials, and emotional restraint. A new chapter begins, honoring a century of heritage while charting a distinctly modern course.

The Icons

Timeless Creations

1947

The Bamboo Bag

Born from wartime scarcity, artisans bent heated bamboo cane into the bag's signature curved handles. What began as resourceful innovation became an enduring symbol of Gucci's ability to transform the unexpected into the extraordinary.

2016

GG Marmont

The modern Gucci icon. Defined by its chevron-pattern matelasse leather and the antique gold-toned Double G hardware, the Marmont collection embodies Michele-era glamour — a bridge between vintage sophistication and contemporary desire.

1953

Horsebit Loafer

A gilt metal snaffle bit across the vamp of a leather loafer — a gesture that redefined casual luxury footwear forever. Worn by Hollywood icons and heads of state alike, it remains one of fashion's most imitated and never surpassed creations.

1961

The Jackie

Originally a simple hobo bag, it earned immortality when Jackie Kennedy Onassis was photographed carrying it across the globe. Reissued across decades, the Jackie's slouchy silhouette and piston-lock closure remain a testament to understated power.

2015

Dionysus

Named for the Greek god of wine and revelry, the Dionysus features a distinctive tiger-head closure — a nod to mythology where the tiger carried Dionysus across the river Tigris. An Alessandro Michele masterpiece that embodies eclectic luxury.

1966

Flora Print

Commissioned by Rodolfo Gucci as a gift for Princess Grace of Monaco, artist Vittorio Accornero created a fantastical garden of flowers, insects, and berries on silk. The Flora print transcended fashion to become art — one of the most reproduced and beloved motifs in luxury history.
